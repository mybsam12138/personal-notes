# Why “Head Resonance” Is Not True Acoustic Resonance

## 1. Short Answer

> “Head resonance” (the feeling) is **not true acoustic resonance in the head**.  
> The **real resonance occurs in the vocal tract (air cavities)**, while the head/face sensations come mainly from **bone and tissue vibration (bone conduction)**.

---

## 2. What Counts as True (Acoustic) Resonance

**Definition:**
- Resonance is the amplification of specific frequencies when **sound waves in air** interact with a cavity.

**Where it happens in the voice:**
- Pharynx (throat)
- Oral cavity (mouth)
- Sometimes nasal cavity

**Key properties:**
- Involves **airborne sound waves**
- Depends on **cavity shape, size, and impedance**
- Produces measurable changes in the spectrum (formants)

---

## 3. What You Feel as “Head Resonance”

**Source of the sensation:**
- Vibrations generated at the vocal folds are transmitted through:
  - Skull bones
  - Facial bones
  - Soft tissues

**Resulting sensations (the “mask”):**
- Nose bridge
- Cheekbones
- Forehead

**Important:**
- These sensations are primarily due to **bone/tissue conduction**, not air resonance inside the skull.

---

## 4. Why It’s Called “Head Resonance” Anyway

- In vocal pedagogy, the term is **perceptual/teaching language**.
- Singers associate **forward/head sensations** with efficient production.
- The label helps guide coordination, even if it’s not strictly physical.

---

## 5. Correct Physical Model (Step-by-Step)

1. **Source:** Vocal folds vibrate → create sound waves
2. **Filter (true resonance):** Vocal tract shapes/amplifies frequencies
3. **Transmission (sensation):** Vibrations travel through bone/tissue → felt in the head

---

## 6. Common Misunderstandings

❌ “Sound is sent into the head”  
❌ “Airflow goes into the skull for resonance”  
❌ “More nasal airflow = better head resonance”

**Corrections:**
- Airflow primarily exits through the **mouth** (soft palate typically raised for vowels)
- The **head is not a primary acoustic resonator** for singing
- The **feeling** in the head is not the **site of resonance**

---

## 7. Practical Implications for Singing

- Aim for **efficient vocal fold vibration** and **well-shaped vocal tract**
- Keep the throat **relaxed and open**
- Allow **forward sensations** to appear naturally
- Avoid pushing air into the nose to “create” head resonance

---

## 8. Final Summary

> “Head resonance” is a **sensation**, not a location of true resonance.  
> Real acoustic resonance occurs in the **air-filled vocal tract**, while the head/face feelings arise from **vibration transmitted through bones and tissues**.

---
