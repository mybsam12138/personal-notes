# 🎤 Voice Types Summary for Pop Singing

## Overview
This guide summarizes the three main voice types used in pop singing:
- Chest Voice
- Head Voice
- Falsetto

Focus: Pitch range, characteristics, and practical usage.

---

## 1. Chest Voice 

### Pitch Range
- Low to mid range

### Sound Characteristics
- Strong
- Full
- Natural (similar to speaking voice)

### Usage in Pop
- Verses
- Emotional and storytelling parts
- Lower notes

### Advantages
- Powerful and stable
- Easy to control for beginners

### Limitations
- Can become strained if pushed too high

---

## 2. Head Voice

### Pitch Range
- Mid-high to high range

### Sound Characteristics
- Light but supported
- Smooth and round tone
- Not airy (compared to falsetto)

### Usage in Pop
- Pre-chorus and chorus
- Higher notes
- Smooth transitions

### Advantages
- Good control
- Safer for high notes
- Essential for mixed voice

### Limitations
- Requires training to stabilize

---

## 3. Falsetto 

### Pitch Range
- High range

### Sound Characteristics
- Very light
- Airy (breathy)
- Thin and soft

### Usage in Pop
- Soft emotional parts
- Stylistic effect
- R&B / soft pop

### Advantages
- Easy access to high notes
- Expressive

### Limitations
- Less power
- Less control compared to head voice

---

## 🎯 Comparison Table

| Feature | Chest Voice | Head Voice | Falsetto |
|--------|------------|------------|----------|
| Pitch Range | Low–Mid | Mid–High | High |
| Strength | Strong | Medium | Light |
| Tone | Full | Round | Airy |
| Control | Easy | Medium | Lower |
| Usage | Verses | Chorus | Style |

---

## 🎵 Practical Learning Strategy for Pop Singing

### Step 1 (Beginner)
- Focus on chest voice
- Build pitch accuracy

### Step 2 (Intermediate)
- Add head voice
- Practice smooth transitions

### Step 3 (Advanced)
- Use falsetto for style and expression

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

- Chest voice = foundation
- Head voice = high-note control
- Falsetto = stylistic color

---

## ✅ Simple Summary

For pop singing:
- Use chest voice for strength
- Use head voice for higher notes
- Use falsetto for soft emotional effect
