# Pitch Control & Vocal Cord Mechanics

## 1. How Vocal Cords Influence Pitch

Pitch is primarily controlled by the **vocal cords ** inside the larynx.

### 1.1 Key Factors

#### (1) Tension 
- Higher tension → faster vibration → higher pitch
- Lower tension → slower vibration → lower pitch

#### (2) Thickness 
- Thinner cords → higher pitch
- Thicker cords → lower pitch

#### (3) Effective Length
- Slight stretching increases effective vibrating length
- Contributes to pitch control

---

### 1.2 Core Principle

👉 **Pitch = vibration frequency of vocal cords**

👉 **Frequency depends on tension + thickness + length**

---

### 1.3 Important Clarification

- Pitch is controlled at the **vocal cord level**
- NOT directly controlled by:
    - throat shape
    - mouth shape
    - nasal cavity

---

## 2. Role of Resonance (Not Pitch)

The vocal tract :
- throat
- mouth
- nasal cavity

👉 affects:
- **timbre**
- NOT pitch

---

## 3. Why High Pitch Requires Tension

To sing higher notes:
- vocal cords must:
    - become **thinner**
    - become **more stretched**
    - increase **tension**

👉 This is **necessary and correct**

---

## 4. Why Some People Feel Tight at High Pitch

### 4.1 Incorrect Coordination

Beginners often:

- squeeze throat
- push too much air
- tense tongue or jaw
- lift larynx excessively

👉 Result:
- extra (unnecessary) tension
- blocked airflow
- strained feeling

---

### 4.2 What Actually Happens

- airflow is restricted
- pressure builds up
- body compensates by pushing harder

👉 This creates:
- strain
- instability
- fatigue

---

## 5. Why Some People Sing High Notes Easily

### 5.1 Efficient Coordination

Skilled singers:

- use **only necessary vocal cord tension**
- keep throat relatively relaxed
- maintain stable airflow

---

### 5.2 Key Characteristics

- vocal cords:
    - thin
    - stretched
    - well-closed (not over-compressed)

- airflow:
    - steady
    - controlled

- surrounding muscles:
    - relaxed

---

### 5.3 Result

👉 High pitch + low effort  
👉 Stable + clear sound  
👉 No tightness feeling

---

## 6. Key Distinction: Tension vs Tightness

| Concept        | Meaning                         | Result        |
|----------------|----------------------------------|---------------|
| Tension        | Necessary cord stretching        | Good ✅       |
| Tightness      | Extra muscle squeezing           | Bad ❌        |

---

## 7. Common Mistake

❌ Thinking:
- "Higher pitch requires squeezing"

✅ Correct:
- "Higher pitch requires efficient cord tension"

---

## 8. Final Model

👉 Vocal cords → control pitch  
👉 Vocal tract → shapes sound  
👉 Whole system → determines efficiency

---

## 9. One Sentence Summary

👉 **High pitch comes from vocal cord tension, not from throat tightness — good singers isolate the tension in the cords while keeping everything else relaxed.**
