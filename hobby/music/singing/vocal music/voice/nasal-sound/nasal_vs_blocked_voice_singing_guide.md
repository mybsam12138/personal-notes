# Nasal vs Blocked Voice (Singing Guide)

## 1. Core Question

Why does “nasal sound” sometimes mean:
- air goes through the nose (normal nasal)
- but also when the nose is blocked (stuffy voice)?

👉 These are actually **two opposite conditions**.

---

## 2. Two Types of “Nasal” Sounds

### 2.1 True Nasal Sound (Nasal Resonance)

**Condition:**
- Soft palate lowered
- Airflow enters nasal cavity

**Result:**
- Nasal cavity participates in resonance
- Sound has nasal quality

**Examples:**
- M / N / NG

✅ This is the real definition of **nasal sound**

---

### 2.2 Blocked Nose (Hyponasal)

**Condition:**
- Nasal cavity blocked (cold, congestion)
- Air cannot enter nose

**Result:**
- Sound becomes:
  - muffled
  - dull
  - congested

❌ This is NOT nasal sound

👉 This is called:
- **Hyponasal (nasal deficiency)**

---

## 3. Key Comparison

| Type | Nasal Cavity | Airflow | Sound Quality |
|------|-------------|--------|--------------|
| Nasal | Open | Goes into nose | Nasal tone |
| Hyponasal | Blocked | Cannot enter nose | Muffled / dull |

---

## 4. Why This Feels Confusing

In daily language:

👉 “nasal” often means:
- any unpleasant voice

But technically:
- Nasal = too much nasal airflow
- Hyponasal = not enough nasal airflow

👉 They are **opposites**

---

## 5. Role of Soft Palate

The soft palate controls airflow direction:

- **Raised** → airflow goes to mouth
- **Lowered** → airflow goes to nose

---

## 6. Application in Singing

### ❌ Avoid 1: Too Nasal
- Too much airflow into nose
- Sound becomes thin / squeezed

### ❌ Avoid 2: Too Blocked (Hyponasal)
- Sound becomes dull / heavy
- Lack of resonance

---

## 7. Correct Balance

👉 Good singing =

- Airflow mainly through **mouth**
- Nasal cavity **not dominant**
- Open and relaxed resonance

---

## 8. Simple Test

1. Sing a vowel ("ah")
2. Pinch your nose

- No change → good (not nasal)
- Sound changes → too nasal

---

## 9. Final Summary

👉 “Nasal sound” and “blocked nose sound” are not the same.

- Nasal = air goes into nose
- Blocked = air cannot go into nose

👉 Good singing requires:

**Oral resonance + controlled airflow + minimal nasal interference**
