# 🎼 Music Types (Pure Classification)

## 1. Genre (Style-Based Classification)

### Definition
Music categorized by style, culture, and sound.

### Major Genres
- Pop
- Rock
- Jazz
- Classical
- Hip-Hop / Rap
- Electronic (EDM)
- R&B (Rhythm and Blues)
- Folk
- Country
- Blues

### Notes
- Most modern music belongs to one or more genres
- Genres can mix (e.g., Pop Rock, Jazz Fusion)

---

## 2. Form (Structure-Based Classification)

### Definition
Music categorized by its internal structure and composition rules.

### Common Forms
- Symphony
- Sonata
- Concerto
- Opera
- Fugue
- Waltz
- Suite

### Notes
- Mostly used in classical music
- Focus on how music is organized

---

## 3. Function (Purpose-Based Classification)

### Definition
Music categorized by its usage or purpose.

### Types
- Dance Music
- Film / Soundtrack Music
- Religious Music
- Military Music
- Background Music (BGM)
- National Anthems

### Notes
- Same music can belong to multiple functions

---

## 4. Historical Period (Era-Based Classification)

### Definition
Music categorized by time period and historical development.

### Main Periods
- Medieval (before 1400)
- Renaissance (1400–1600)
- Baroque (1600–1750)
- Classical (1750–1820)
- Romantic (1820–1900)
- Modern / Contemporary (1900–present)

### Notes
- Each period has distinct musical characteristics

---

## 5. Instrumentation (Performance-Based Classification)

### Definition
Music categorized by performers or instruments.

### Types
- Vocal Music (with singing)
- Instrumental Music (no singing)
- Solo
- Ensemble
- Orchestra
- Choir

---

# 🎯 Key Understanding

Music can be classified in multiple dimensions at the same time.

Example:
- A Pop song (Genre)
- With Vocal (Instrumentation)
- Used in a Movie (Function)

---

# 🧠 Summary

Music types are not a single system.

They are a combination of:
- Style (Genre)
- Structure (Form)
- Purpose (Function)
- Time (Era)
- Performance (Instrumentation)
