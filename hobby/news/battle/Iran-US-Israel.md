# Iran–US–Israel Conflict (2025–2026) – Overview

## 1. Background: Why This War Happened

### 1.1 Long-Term Causes
- **Historical hostility**: Since the 1979 Iranian Revolution, Iran and the United States have been geopolitical rivals.
- **Nuclear issue**: The U.S. and its allies believe Iran may develop nuclear weapons, while Iran denies this.
- **Regional power struggle**: Iran supports groups like Hezbollah and other militias, while the U.S. supports Israel and Gulf allies.
- **Israel–Iran rivalry**: Israel sees Iran as an existential threat, especially due to missile programs and proxy forces.

### 1.2 Immediate Causes (2025–2026)
- Breakdown of diplomatic negotiations over Iran’s nuclear program.
- Escalation after the 2025 Israel–Iran conflict (12-day war).
- Growing military tensions and proxy conflicts across the Middle East.

---

## 2. How the War Started

- **February 28, 2026**: The United States and Israel launched large-scale airstrikes on Iran’s nuclear facilities, military bases, and leadership targets. 
- Iran responded quickly with missile and drone attacks against Israel and U.S. interests. 
- The conflict rapidly expanded across the region, involving air battles, cyber operations, and proxy forces.

---

## 3. Major Developments (Timeline)

### Early Phase
- U.S. and Israel targeted Iran’s nuclear infrastructure and military capabilities.
- Iran retaliated with missile strikes and regional proxy attacks.

### Escalation
- Closure of the **Strait of Hormuz**, disrupting global oil supply.
- Air battles, including U.S. aircraft shot down by Iran.
- Spread of conflict across multiple fronts in the Middle East.

### Current Situation (April 2026)
- Ongoing war with heavy airstrikes and economic disruption.
- Diplomatic efforts for ceasefire are being discussed but not finalized. 

---

## 4. What Each Side Wants

### United States & Israel
- Prevent Iran from developing nuclear weapons.
- Destroy Iran’s missile and military capabilities.
- Reduce Iran’s regional influence and proxy networks.
- Ensure security of Israel and allies.

### Iran
- Preserve regime survival and sovereignty.
- Maintain or advance its strategic deterrence (missiles, nuclear capability).
- Expel U.S. influence from the Middle East.
- Strengthen regional alliances and proxies.

---

## 5. Role of Syria

- Syria is **not directly involved** in the war but is affected by it.
- It hosts Iran-backed forces and is geographically strategic.
- Syrian leadership has stated it will avoid joining unless attacked.

---

## 6. Consequences of the War

### 6.1 Regional Impact
- Thousands of casualties and widespread destruction.
- Increased instability across the Middle East.
- Risk of a broader regional war.

### 6.2 Global Impact
- Oil prices surged (over 50% increase). 
- Disruption of global trade routes (especially Hormuz).
- Rising inflation and economic uncertainty worldwide.

### 6.3 Military Impact
- Demonstrates limits of air superiority.
- Increased use of drones, missiles, and hybrid warfare.

---

## 7. Future Outlook (Analysis)

### Short-Term (0–3 months)
- Continued airstrikes and limited ground escalation.
- Strong push for ceasefire negotiations.

### Medium-Term (3–12 months)
- Possible negotiated settlement involving:
    - Sanctions relief
    - Nuclear restrictions
    - Security guarantees
- Or prolonged regional conflict if negotiations fail.

### Long-Term Risks
- A **wider Middle East war** involving multiple countries.
- Permanent instability in global energy markets.
- Increased militarization and arms race in the region.

---

## 8. Overall Judgment

This conflict is:
- **Not just a single war**, but part of a long-term geopolitical struggle.
- Driven by **security fears, ideology, and regional dominance**.
- Likely to end either through:
    - A negotiated compromise, or
    - A prolonged cycle of escalation and temporary ceasefires.

**Key Insight:**  
Even if the war ends soon, the underlying conflict between Iran and the U.S./Israel will likely continue for years.
