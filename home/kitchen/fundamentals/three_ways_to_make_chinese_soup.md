# Three Ways to Make Chinese Soup

This note compares three common ways to make Chinese soup: **double boiling**, **low-heat stovetop simmering**, and **electric pot cooking**. The main differences are temperature, cooking time, water loss, and soup result.

---

## 1. Double Boiling 

### Heating method
The soup is placed in an **inner pot**, while water is heated in the **outer pot**. Heat is transferred gently through the hot water.

### Temperature
- Outer water: usually close to **95–100°C**
- Soup inside the inner pot: usually about **85–95°C**

### Cooking time
- Usually **1.5–3 hours**
- Small portions are common

### Effect on the soup
- The broth is usually **clearer**
- The flavor is **gentle, clean, and refined**
- Water loss is **very low**
- Good for herbal soups and delicate soups

### Best for
- Soft-shell turtle soup
- Herbal soup
- Bird's nest
- Sweet soup desserts

---

## 2. Low-Heat Stovetop Simmering 

### Heating method
The soup pot sits directly on the stove. It is usually brought to a boil first, then cooked over low heat for a long time.

### Temperature
- Usually around **95–100°C**
- Can remain at a steady simmer or light boil

### Cooking time
- Usually **1.5–4 hours**
- Sometimes longer for traditional Cantonese soups

### Effect on the soup
- The broth is usually **richer and deeper in flavor**
- Water loss is **the highest**
- The soup may become **cloudier** if boiled too hard
- Best for extracting flavor from meat and bones

### Best for
- Cantonese old fire soup
- Pork rib soup
- Chicken soup
- Bone-based soups

---

## 3. Electric Pot Cooking / Electric Soup Pot 

### Heating method
The pot is heated directly by electricity. It works like a controlled simmering pot.

### Temperature
- Usually around **90–100°C**
- Depends on the appliance and mode

### Cooking time
- Usually **1.5–3 hours**
- Often similar to stovetop soup, but more convenient

### Effect on the soup
- Flavor is usually **stable and reliable**
- Water loss is **moderate**
- More convenient than stovetop cooking
- The soup is often **less intense than stovetop simmering**, but stronger than double boiling

### Best for
- Daily home soup cooking
- Busy schedules
- Simple meat and herbal soups

---

# Quick Comparison

| Method | Typical Temperature | Cooking Time | Water Loss | Soup Result |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Double boiling | 85–95°C in soup | 1.5–3 h | Very low | Clearest, gentlest |
| Low-heat stovetop simmering | 95–100°C | 1.5–4 h | High | Richest, deepest |
| Electric pot cooking | 90–100°C | 1.5–3 h | Moderate | Convenient, balanced |

---

# Simple Summary

- **Double boiling**: gentlest heat, clearest broth, least water loss  
- **Low-heat stovetop simmering**: strongest extraction, richest flavor, most water loss  
- **Electric pot cooking**: easiest daily method, balanced result, moderate water loss
