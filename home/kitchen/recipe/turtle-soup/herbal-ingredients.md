# Chinese Turtle Soup Herbal Ingredients Overview

This document summarizes the dried ingredients included in a traditional Chinese herbal soup package often used for cooking turtle soup or slow-cooked nourishing soups.

---

# Ingredient List

## 1. Red Dates (Jujube)
**English name:** Red Date / Jujube  
**Scientific name:** Ziziphus jujuba

### Description
Red dates are commonly used in Chinese soups and herbal cooking. They are often sliced with the pit removed, leaving a round ring-shaped piece.

### Functions
- Adds natural sweetness to the soup
- Balances herbal flavors
- Traditionally believed to nourish blood and energy

### Usage
Place directly into the soup pot during cooking. No soaking is required.

---

## 2. Longan Meat
**English name:** Dried Longan / Longan Aril  
**Scientific name:** Dimocarpus longan

### Description
Longan meat is the dried flesh of the longan fruit with the seed removed. It appears as small golden or amber pieces.

### Functions
- Adds mild sweetness
- Enhances the aroma of the soup
- Traditionally believed to support energy and circulation

### Usage
Add directly into the soup while simmering.

---

## 3. Lotus Seeds
**English name:** Lotus Seed  
**Scientific name:** Nelumbo nucifera

### Description
Lotus seeds are small round white seeds often used in Chinese soups and desserts.

### Functions
- Adds mild sweetness and texture
- Traditionally believed to support digestion and calm the body

### Usage
Add directly to the soup during cooking.

---

## 4. Dried Tangerine Peel
**English name:** Dried Tangerine Peel / Aged Tangerine Peel  
**Chinese medicine name:** Chenpi  
**Scientific name:** Citrus reticulata

### Description
Chenpi is aged dried citrus peel commonly used in Chinese cuisine and herbal soups.

### Functions
- Removes unwanted odors from meat
- Reduces greasiness
- Adds a citrus fragrance to the broth

### Usage
Rinse briefly with water before adding to soup.

---

## 5. Radix Millettiae (Niu Da Li)
**English name:** Radix Millettiae / Niu Da Li Root  
**Scientific name:** Millettia speciosa

### Description
This root is a common herbal soup ingredient in southern Chinese cooking.

### Functions
- Adds herbal aroma
- Traditionally believed to support joints and vitality
- Often used in slow-cooked nourishing soups

### Usage
Add directly into the soup and simmer for a long period to extract flavor.

---

# Typical Soup Combination

A traditional herbal turtle soup may include the following combination:

- Red Dates
- Longan Meat
- Lotus Seeds
- Dried Tangerine Peel
- Radix Millettiae (Niu Da Li Root)
- Fresh Ginger (optional)

---

# Basic Cooking Method

1. Place the turtle meat into a pot.
2. Add all dried herbal ingredients.
3. Add approximately 1.5–2 liters of water.
4. Bring to a boil.
5. Reduce to low heat and simmer for 1.5–2 hours.
6. Add salt near the end of cooking.

---

# Storage

## Dried Ingredients
- Store in a cool and dry place.
- Can also be stored in the refrigerator for long-term storage.

## Fresh Meat
- Refrigerate if cooking within 1–2 days.
- Freeze if storing longer.

---

# Notes

These ingredients are typical of Cantonese-style nourishing soups and create a balanced broth with mild sweetness and herbal aroma.
