# Oven Usage Guide (Rack, Baking Tray, and Aluminum Foil)

This guide explains the basic use of an oven, focusing on **oven rack**, **baking tray**, and **aluminum foil**, using **chicken wings** and **beef patties** as examples.

---

# 1. Main Oven Components

Most ovens include these parts:

### Oven Rack
A metal grid used to hold food.

Characteristics:
- Allows hot air to circulate around food
- Oil and fat can drip down
- Helps food become crisp

Best for:
- Chicken wings
- Meat
- Toasting
- Grilling-style cooking

---

### Baking Tray
A flat metal tray placed under or used for cooking.

Characteristics:
- Collects oil and drippings
- Provides a stable flat surface
- Can hold foil or baking paper

Uses:
- Cooking food directly
- Placing under the rack to catch oil

---

### Aluminum Foil (Tin Foil)
Thin metal sheet used for cooking.

Common purposes:
- Prevent sticking
- Collect oil
- Reduce cleaning effort

Important note:
- Do **not completely wrap food when roasting**, or it will steam instead of roast.

---

# 2. Common Oven Setup Methods

## Method 1: Food on Baking Tray + Foil

Structure:

Food  
Aluminum Foil  
Baking Tray

Advantages:
- Easy cleanup
- Oil stays on foil

Disadvantages:
- Bottom may become oily
- Food may stick if cheese melts

Example:
- Cheese beef patties

---

## Method 2: Food on Oven Rack (Recommended for meat)

Structure:

Food  
Oven Rack  
Baking Tray (to catch oil)

Advantages:
- Better airflow
- Crispier surface
- Less sticking

Best for:
- Chicken wings
- Steak
- Burgers

Recommended approach.

---

# 3. Example: Cooking Chicken Wings

### Setup
Place chicken wings on:

Oven Rack  
Baking Tray underneath (to catch oil)

Optional:
- Put foil on the tray for easier cleaning.

### Oven Settings

Temperature:
200°C

Cooking time:
25 minutes total

Steps:

1. Preheat oven to **200°C for 5 minutes**
2. Place wings on rack
3. Bake **15 minutes**
4. Flip wings
5. Bake **8–10 minutes**

Result:
- Crispy outside
- Juicy inside

---

# 4. Example: Cooking Cheese Beef Patties

### Setup

Option A (easy cleanup)

Beef Patty  
Foil  
Baking Tray

Option B (less sticking)

Beef Patty  
Oven Rack  
Baking Tray below

### Oven Settings

Temperature:
200°C

Cooking time:
12–15 minutes

Signs it is ready:
- Cheese fully melted
- Surface slightly browned
- Oil begins to release

Avoid cooking longer than **18 minutes**, or the patty may become dry.

---

# 5. Tips for Using Aluminum Foil

Foil can be folded into a shallow tray.

Example:

┌────────────┐  
│            │  
│   food     │  
│            │  
└────────────┘

Benefits:
- Prevents oil spilling
- Keeps tray clean
- Prevents foil from moving in convection ovens

Foil sides up about **1–2 cm** is sufficient.

---

# 6. Quick Summary

Best setup for crispy food:

Food  
Oven Rack  
Baking Tray (with foil optional)

Best setup for easy cleaning:

Food  
Foil  
Baking Tray

Typical oven process:

1. Preheat oven
2. Place food on middle rack
3. Cook at recommended temperature
4. Flip halfway if needed
5. Remove when surface browns
