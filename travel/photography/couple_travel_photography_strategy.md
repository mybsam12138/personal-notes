# Travel Photography Strategy -- Couple Travel System

## 1. Core Philosophy

Travel photography should balance:

-   Geographic identity (Where are we?)
-   Emotional memory (We were here together.)
-   Spatial depth (Does the image feel immersive?)

The goal is not quantity, but structured coverage.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 2. Recommended Shot Structure Per Location

## Minimum Safe Structure (3--4 shots)

At each meaningful location:

1.  **Landscape Shot**
    -   Pure environmental record
    -   Establishes location identity
2.  **Her Portrait**
    -   Prioritize expression and light
    -   Clean background, soft light
3.  **Your Portrait**
    -   Optional lower priority
    -   Can be simplified
4.  **Couple Photo (If possible)**
    -   Highest emotional value
    -   Use timer, support surface, or ask for help

If time or conditions are limited: - Prioritize couple shot → her
portrait → landscape.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 3. How to Choose a Photo Spot

A strong shooting position should satisfy at least 3 of the following:

1.  **Iconic Element Present**
    -   Recognizable landmark
    -   Unique terrain
    -   Signature architecture
    -   Distinctive natural formation
2.  **Good Light Direction**
    -   Side light preferred
    -   Soft front light acceptable
    -   Avoid harsh top-down noon light
    -   Use backlight only for silhouette mood
3.  **Clean Background**
    -   Minimal clutter
    -   Limited random tourists
    -   Clear visual separation
4.  **Spatial Layering**
    -   Foreground
    -   Midground (subject)
    -   Background

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 4. Understanding Foreground--Midground--Background

## Important Principle

Foreground/midground/background refers to **relative visual distance**,
not physical measurement.

### Typical Practical Ranges

-   Foreground: 0.3m -- 2m from camera
-   Midground: 2m -- 8m (usually subjects)
-   Background: 10m to infinity

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## What Each Layer Should Contain

### Foreground

Purpose: Add depth and dimensionality

Can include: - Rocks - Edges - Water surfaces - Framing elements
(branches, windows) - Subtle textures

Should not: - Dominate the frame excessively - Distract from main
subject

Foreground does NOT need to be blurred. Depth matters more than blur.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Midground

Purpose: Primary subject zone

Typically: - Couple standing naturally - One person interacting with
environment - Positioned with distance from background

Avoid: - Standing too close to camera (distortion) - Standing flat
against background (no depth)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Background

Purpose: Geographic identity and atmosphere

Should contain: - Landmark - Terrain pattern - Horizon - Sky -
Environmental context

The background defines location memory.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 5. Wide vs Telephoto Strategy

## Wide Angle (Recommended for depth)

-   Strong spatial layering
-   Immersive feel
-   Emphasizes environment scale
-   Better for couple storytelling

## Telephoto (Compression Style)

-   Flattens distance
-   Emphasizes texture and repetition
-   Good for terrain detail
-   Less immersive spatial depth

Choose based on intention: - Depth and story → wide - Structure and
texture → telephoto

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 6. Emotional Hierarchy

In long-term memory value:

1.  Couple photo (highest emotional weight)
2.  Her portrait
3.  Environmental landscape
4.  Individual self-portrait

Landscapes can be revisited. Moments together cannot.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 7. Advanced Refinement

To elevate an image:

-   Maintain physical separation between subject and background
-   Use slight side light for dimensionality
-   Include subtle foreground to create depth
-   Avoid central static positioning
-   Encourage natural interaction instead of rigid posing

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 8. Practical On-Site Workflow

At each location:

Step 1 -- Identify iconic background\
Step 2 -- Evaluate light direction\
Step 3 -- Check for clean layering\
Step 4 -- Capture couple shot (if possible)\
Step 5 -- Capture individual portraits\
Step 6 -- Capture pure landscape

This structure prevents regret while preserving travel flow.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Final Insight

A strong travel photography system is not about chasing perfection. It
is about structured coverage, emotional prioritization, and spatial
awareness.

If the location is recognizable, the light is intentional, and the
moment feels real --- the photograph succeeds.
