# Heimlich Maneuver (Abdominal Thrust) — Quick Guide

## 1. What It Is

The Heimlich Maneuver is a first aid technique used to help someone who is choking due to a blocked airway.

👉 Goal:
- Create a strong airflow from the lungs
- Expel the object blocking the airway

---

## 2. Core Principle

> It is NOT about squeezing the object directly.

👉 It works by:
- Compressing the abdomen
- Pushing the diaphragm upward
- Forcing air out of the lungs
- Blasting the object out of the airway

---

## 3. When to Use It

### ❌ Do NOT use if:
- The person can cough
- The person can speak
- The person can breathe

👉 Action: Encourage them to keep coughing

---

### 🚨 Use it immediately if:
- Cannot speak
- Cannot breathe
- Cannot cough effectively
- Grabbing throat (universal choking sign)

---

## 4. Steps (For Adults)

1. Stand behind the person
2. Wrap your arms around their waist
3. Make a fist
4. Place it:
   - Above the navel
   - Below the ribcage (upper abdomen)
5. Grab your fist with the other hand
6. Perform quick thrusts:
   - Inward + upward
7. Repeat until the object is expelled

---

## 5. Self-Rescue

If alone:

- Use a chair, table, or edge
- Press upper abdomen against it
- Perform strong inward-upward thrusts

---

## 6. Key Points to Remember

- Correct position: **upper abdomen (not chest)**
- Direction: **inward + upward**
- Use force: quick and firm thrusts

---

## 7. Common Mistakes

❌ Pressing the chest instead of abdomen
❌ Using weak force
❌ Performing when the person can still cough

---

## 8. Risks vs Benefit

- Possible risks:
  - Rib injury
  - Internal discomfort

👉 But:

> Not acting during complete choking can be fatal within minutes.

---

## 9. One Key Rule

> If they can cough, let them cough.
> If they cannot breathe, act immediately.

---

## 10. Final Summary

The Heimlich Maneuver is a life-saving technique that uses abdominal thrusts to generate airflow and remove airway obstruction. Even if not perfect, performing it correctly can save a life in emergencies.
